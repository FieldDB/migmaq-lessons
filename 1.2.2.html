Coping: how to do your language-learning IN the language

This particular lesson is offered right from the start, because it is most effective for learners to learn the language THROUGH the language. By giving them the crucial phrases for this early on, we aim to encourage doing this as much as possible from the getgo, instead of dropping back to English. Learners of course naturally drop back to English, but so do speakers, since it is the language they have used with non-speakers for years. A successful learner has to overcome not only their own lifelong habits of using English, but also has to find polite, respectful ways of turning the conversation from English back to Mi'gmaq wherever reasonably possible. These phrases are one of the key ways they can do this.

Ge' nnu'-gluli.

Please speak to me in Native.

Tali-ulm'tu'n 'nnuigtug gluli.

Would you be so kind as to talk to me in Native?

Ge' app tlua.

Please say it again.

Sangew tlua.

Say it slowly.

Sangewi'si.

Speak slowly.

Menaqa tlua.

Say it carefully/precisely.
