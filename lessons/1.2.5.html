Identifying siblings overall

This lesson introduces plural 'who' (not found directly in English), and presents wijigumjig as an unexplained 'your siblings/brothers and sisters'. An expanded version of this lesson might have an answer using wijigumgig 'my..., those who I...'. This would at least introduce that basic 'I ... them', 'you ... them' pattern. Here however, drawing too much attention to this pattern might overload learners.

Wenig wijigumjig?

Who are your brothers and sisters?

- Mise'l, Sa'n, Malia'n, aq Mali.

Michael, John, Mary-Ann, and Mary.
