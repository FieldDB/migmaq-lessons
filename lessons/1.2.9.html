Identifying deceased relatives

Based on previous lessons' kinship terms, we introduce the absentative (= deceased, no longer present) for people/animates: -aq. The P-past -p(n)- and the S-past -s(n)- both naturally come together with the absentative in this context. Not necessary to go into details on this for now with learners. But it is worth being prepared to explain that the P-past is used for a direct perspective on the past (the usual, "I saw it happen myself, or know it for sure"), and the S-past tends to be used for indirect perspective on the past, and in particular, for most questions about the past, as here.

Taluisisnaq gmijgamijaq?

What was your (deceased) grandfather's name?

- Nmijgamijaq Sulian teluisipnaq.

My (deceased) grandfather's name was Julian.

Taluisisnaq gugmijaq?

What was your (deceased) grandmother's name?

- Nugmijaq 'Lluis teluisipnaq.

My (deceased) grandmother's name was Eloise.
