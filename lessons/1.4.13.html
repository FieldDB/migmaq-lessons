How many things do you see? Large numbers.

Here we combine the 'see' patterns with the larger numbers for counting things. The larger numbers have been intentionally selected for easy visual/image presentation. Hence "36" as editing a single image of a rock into a 6x6 configuration.

Ta's'gl nemitu'nn mutputi'l?

How many chairs do you see?

- Nemituann lluigneg te's'gl mutputi'l.

I see seven chairs.

Ta's'gl nemitu'nn mutputi'l?

How many chairs do you see?

- Nemituann tapuisga'ql patauti'l.

I see twenty tables.

Ta's'gl nemitu'nn mutputi'l?

How many chairs do you see?

- Nemituann ne'sisga'ql jel asugom gun'tal.

I see thirty-six rocks.

Ta's'gl nemitu'nn mutputi'l?

How many chairs do you see?

- Nemituann newtisga'qal jel ne'w l'gusuaqann.

I see fourteen ladders.

Ta's'gl nemitu'nn mutputi'l?

How many chairs do you see?

- Nemituann newisga'ql jel ta'pu ga'qann.

I see forty-two doors.
