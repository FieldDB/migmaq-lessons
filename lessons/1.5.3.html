How old are you? (Conversation)

Taking the form of a single unbroken conversation, this lesson starts from the basic opposites gisigui- 'be old' and maljewejui- 'be young', and uses them to introduces two ways to say '(more)...than...': - me' ... aq ... - me' ... jemu ... It also introduces the related idea of 'so (much)...', expressed by te'uji- / tew'ji-. And also the very different sense of 'so, then ...' that the word na is used for.

Ta'si-puna'n?

How old are you?

- Tapuisgegi-puna'i jel newt.

I'm twenty-one.

O', me' maljewejuin!

Oh, you're still young!

- 'Njignam metla'si-puna't. Gnegg el-maljewejuit aq ni'n.

My younger brother is ten. He's far younger than me.

Na gi'l me' gisiguin jemu negm?

So you're older than him?

- E'e, te'uji-gisigui a....

Yes, I'm so old....
