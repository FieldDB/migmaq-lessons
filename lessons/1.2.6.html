Identifying siblings by relation

Here the 'what is my/your name?' pattern is extended to show the important 's/he...' ending. At the same time, the full four-way system of [older/younger brother/sister] vs. the English two-way system of [brother/sister] is now laid out directly and completely. In the background here is also the use of word order to highlight relative importance of information: the repeated information is first, and then the key new information (the name asked for) comes right before the verb.

Taluisit 'gsis?

What is your older brother's name?

- 'Nsis Mise'l teluisit.

My older brother's name is Michael.

Taluisit gmis?

What is your older sister's name?

- Nmis Malia'n teluisit.

My older sister's name is Mary-Ann.

Taluisit 'gjignam?

What is your younger brother's name?

- 'Njignam Sa'n teluisit.

My younger brother's name is John.

Taluisit 'ggwe'ji'j?

What is your younger sister's name?

- 'Ngwe'ji'j Mali teluisit.

My younger sister's name is Mary.
