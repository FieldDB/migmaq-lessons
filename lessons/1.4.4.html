How many things do you see?

This lesson combines the earlier nemi'g(ig)/nemitu(ann) pattern with the 'how many ...?' patterns just learned, helping learners see how they can now build more complex sentences. Image files again simply show these numbers: X number of countables together, either as a natural picture or a photoshopping of X number of identical images.

Ta's'gl nemitu'nn mutputi'l?

How many chairs do you see?

- Nemitu newte' mutputi awtigtug.

I see one chairs in the road.

Ta's'gl nemitu'nn mutputi'l?

How many chairs do you see?

- Nemituann tapu'gl mutputi'l awtigtug.

I see two chairs in the road.

Ta's'gl nemitu'nn mutputi'l?

How many chairs do you see?

- Nemituann ne'sisgl mutputi'l awtigtug.

I see three chairs in the road.

Ta's'gl nemitu'nn mutputi'l?

How many chairs do you see?

- Nemituann pugwelgl mutputi'l awtigtug.

I see many chairs.
