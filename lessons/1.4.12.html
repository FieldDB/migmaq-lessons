How many people do you see? Large numbers.

Here we combine the 'see' patterns with the larger numbers for counting people (and similarly-treated words like 'star'). The larger numbers have been intentionally selected for easy visual/image presentation. Hence "25" as editing a single image of a child into a 5x5 configuration.

Ta'sijig nemi'jig ji'nmug?

How many men do you see?

- Nemi'gig asugom te'sijig ji'nmug.

I see six men.

Ta'sijig nemi'jig e'pijig?

How many women do you see?

- Nemi'gig newtisgegsijig e'pijig.

I see ten women.

Ta'sijig nemi'jig mijua'ji'jg?

How many children do you see?

- Nemi'gig tapuisgegsijig jel na'n mijua'ji'jg.

I see twenty-five children.

Ta'sijig nemi'jig gloqowejg?

How many stars do you see?

- Nemi'gig lluigneg te'sisgegsijig jel na'n gloqowejg.

I see seventy-five stars.

Ta'sijig nemi'jig mimajuinu'g?

How many people do you see?

- Nemi'gig pesgunateg te'sisgegsijig jel pesgunateg mimajuinu'g.

I see ninety-nine people.
