Identifying people

This lesson teaches the 'this' and 'that' words of Mi'gmaq, as well as the important question 'who?', and the important /X na Y/ pattern, which acts as an effective equivalent to English 'X is Y, X = Y'. It grounds this in an introduction to part of the four-way system of [older/younger brother/sister] vs. the English two-way system of [brother/sister]. It requires pictures---and would be even better if video---to see the near (ula) and far (ala), and which people the speakers are looking at.

Wen na ula?

Who is this?

- 'Njignam.

My younger brother.

Wen na ala?

Who is that?

- 'Ngwe'ji'j.

My younger sister.

Ula na 'ggwe'ji'j?

Is this your younger sister?

- Moqwa', ula na 'njignam.

No, this is my younger brother.

Ala na 'gjignam?

Is that your younger brother?

- Moqwa', ala na 'ngwe'ji'j.

No, that is my younger sister.
