What's the date today?

Introducing the -uguni-t ending for 'Xth date of the month', building again on number system. Also introducing amgwesewei 'first', na'gweg 'day', and especially, gisgug 'today'. An improved version might take the opportunity to introduce ugtejgewei 'last' in contrast to amgwesewei 'first', if it is in fact natural to say things like ugtejgewei na'gweg 'the last day'. For each, the image is a calendar with the actual date marked on it.

Ta'sugunit gisgug?

What date is it today?

- Gisgug amgwesewei na'gweg.

Today is the first day [of the month].

Ta'sugunit gisgug?

What date is it today?

- Gisgug tapugunit.

Today is the second [day].

Ta'sugunit gisgug?

What date is it today?

- Gisgug nesugunit.

Today is the third day [of the month].

Ta'sugunit gisgug?

What date is it today?

- Gisgug metla'sugunit.

Today is the tenth day [of the month].

Ta'sugunit gisgug?

What date is it today?

- Gisgug tapuisgegigunit jel si'st.

Today is the twenty-third day [of the month].

Ta'sugunit gisgug?

What date is it today?

- Gisgug ne'sisgegigunit jel newt.

Today is the thirty-first day [of the month].
