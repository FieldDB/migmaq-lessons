Unit 1. What is your name and where are you from?

The first thing you’ll need to be able to say in Mi’gmaq is who you are! In this lesson we’ll show you how to say who you are, where you are from and where you live (as well as who your parents are, where they are from and where they live).
  • 1(current)
  • 2(current)
  • 3(current)

Taluisin?

What's your name?

Teluisi Joseph Noel.

My name is Joseph Noel.

Tami tett tleiawin?

Where are you from?

Listugujig tleiawi.

Unit 2. Who are your parents?

Did you notice the pattern? When you are talking about yourself (I / me) there is no ending on the verb teluisi but when are talking about what someone else (you) are doing teluisi needs an n at the end - teluisin.

Unit 3. Where do you and your parents live?

Did you notice the pattern? When you are talking about someone else (he / she) the verb teluisi ends in a t - teluisit. ALSO, When talking about your own family members the person begins in an n but when talking about someone else’s (your) family members the person begins in g - (e.g. nujj vs. gujj).
  • 1(current)
  • 2(current)
  • 3(current)

Tami tett wigin?

Where do you live?

Listugujg wigi.

I live in Listuguj.

Unit 4. Dialogue

In the last unit you should have recognized the pattern that we showed you in the previous two units: verbs about I/me have no ending, verbs about you end in n, verbs about he/she end in t. Also remember that family members belonging to I/me begin with n and that family members belonging to you begin with g.
  • 1(current)

Migmaq transcription

A: Hi, what's your name?

Migmaq transcription

B: Joseph Noel. I am from Listuguj.

Migmaq transcription

A: Really? Do you live in Listuguj?

Migmaq transcription

B: No, but my mother lives in Listuguj.

Migmaq transcription

A: What is your mother's name?

Migmaq transcription

B: Susan Noel. My father's name is Peter Noel.
